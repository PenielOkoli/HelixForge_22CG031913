// Plain English
The non-template (coding) strand has the same sequence as the mRNA — just T→U. The template strand is the mirror that RNA polymerase actually reads, so HelixForge reverses and complements it first. Pick whichever you have.

// What You're Seeing
This model shows B-form DNA — the most common conformation in living cells, discovered by Watson & Crick in 1953. The two coloured ribbons are the sugar-phosphate backbones; each sphere between them is a base pair holding the two strands together via hydrogen bonds. There are approximately 10.5 base pairs per complete helical turn, and the helix is roughly 2 nm wide. The sequence shown here is taken from your most recent analysis (or a demo sequence if you haven't run one yet).
